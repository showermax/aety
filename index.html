Advertising management for e-commerce

Increased the ROI of Digital Advertising X2

industry Online Store (Household Appliances) marketing budget $5,000 business challenge The cost per lead (CPL) is equal to the average cost per order (CPO), therefore, the advertising does not pay off Increased the ROI of Digital Advertising X2 Main concerns First Problem The cost per lead (CPL) is comparable to the cost of the products. Therefore, their advertising campaigns do not pay off. Solution Through the analysis we found the products with the highets CPL. So we just reduce bids on this category. As a result, the CPL is lower, but the conversion stays the same, so therefore a positive ROI is achieved. Second Problem A lot of clicks, but no orders. Non-target audience clicks on the ad campaigns and therefore wasting advertising money. Solution We analyze the keywords stats, then make bids on ad groups with the lowest number of orders lower. For those keywords that bring orders increase bids. As a result, the budget remains the same while the effectiveness of advertising has been increased. Third Problem Paid traffic is being placed in all regions while there are regions with no orders. Therefore, some of the advertising budget is being spent inefficiently. Solution We identify regions with a negative ROI and make bids lower for those regions. Bids for profitable regions, however, should be increased. Fourth Problem During the non-working hours, the number of clicks on advertising remains the same, while the number of sales orders is significantly reduced. Solution We saw that the cost per order (CPO) during off-hours is higher due to the low conversion (CV2) and high cost per clicks (CPC). Therefore, bids should be lowered for the period of time when it is not profitable to run paid ad campaigns. Also, priority for sales managers to process such requests should be increased. As a result, the conversion rate and sales increase.
